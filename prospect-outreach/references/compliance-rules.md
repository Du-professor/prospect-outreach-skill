# Outreach Compliance Rules

Use these rules before drafting outreach and compliance notes. This is operational guidance, not legal advice.

## Privacy and Contact Data

- Use business-relevant public company information and role-based business contacts.
- Do not scrape, expose, or use private personal emails, private phone numbers, home addresses, personal IDs, sensitive personal attributes, or non-public social profiles.
- Do not over-personalize from individual activity, family details, health, politics, religion, ethnicity, union membership, or other sensitive traits.
- If the user provides personal contact data, use it only when it is clearly business-relevant and lawful for the user's context.

## Consent, Opt-out, and Anti-spam

- Do not generate automated bulk outreach, mailbox connection steps, mass scraping flows, or spam-like sending instructions.
- Keep cold outreach targeted, relevant, and low pressure.
- Include a light stop-contact line in final follow-ups or when appropriate, for example:
  - "If this is not relevant, I will not follow up further."
  - "If you are not the right person, I would appreciate being pointed to the appropriate team."
- Do not hide sender identity, use deceptive subject lines, or imply an existing relationship that does not exist.

## Regional Awareness

- For EU/UK prospects, be especially careful with GDPR and ePrivacy expectations: use business relevance, minimal personal data, clear sender identity, and an easy way to stop follow-up.
- For US prospects, avoid deceptive headers, misleading subjects, and missing sender identity in CAN-SPAM-sensitive contexts.
- For Canada, use extra care with CASL-style consent expectations; prefer relationship-building or routing questions over promotional language.
- For China, avoid collecting or exposing personal information beyond what is needed for business contact under personal information protection expectations.

## Sensitive Industries

Use stricter wording for medical, financial, government, defense, public safety, critical infrastructure, education, and regulated utilities:

- Avoid claims about compliance, safety, clinical, financial, security, or public-sector suitability unless directly sourced.
- Ask exploratory technical or routing questions instead of suggesting operational changes.
- Do not mention non-public systems, incidents, vulnerabilities, budgets, or procurement plans.

## Claims and Proof

- Do not fabricate customer logos, partnerships, certifications, case studies, metrics, benchmarks, ROI, savings, or compatibility.
- Do not say the sender has reviewed the prospect's internal systems.
- If evidence is weak, use conditional language: "if relevant", "if your team is evaluating", "I wanted to understand whether".

## Sending Boundaries

- This skill drafts and prioritizes outreach only.
- Do not send emails, connect to email accounts, configure CRM automation, or create bulk campaign workflows.
- For P4 accounts, recommend research or monitoring. Only draft a neutral note if the user explicitly still wants a message.
